# Terra Nova Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Terra Nova is an open, community-driven initiative focused on building high-quality software through shared ownership,
strong collaboration, and modern development practices.

We are committed to creating a community that is welcoming, respectful, constructive, and open to everyone, regardless
of background, identity, experience level, or preferred technology stack.

Terra Nova is not defined by a specific language, platform, or framework. It is defined by how we build software
together.

We want this community to be a place where people can:

- contribute with confidence
- share ideas openly
- receive respectful feedback
- collaborate across differences
- build software that lasts

## Our Vision

Terra Nova exists to help people create something new together.

We value:

- software designed to work well together
- strong standards and best practices
- an automation-first mindset
- open collaboration across individuals, teams, and organizations
- a foundation that evolves through shared effort

This is not just about code. It is about collaboration, responsibility, and continuous improvement.

## Expected Behavior

Members of the Terra Nova community are expected to:

- Be respectful, professional, and constructive in all interactions
- Assume good intent while still giving honest feedback
- Communicate clearly and focus on helping others succeed
- Prefer collaboration over gatekeeping
- Share knowledge openly and help reduce friction for contributors
- Accept feedback with maturity and respond with professionalism
- Take responsibility for the quality and evolution of what they contribute
- Support consistency, maintainability, and pragmatic engineering practices
- Help create an environment where everyone feels welcome to participate

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable in the Terra Nova community:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Personal attacks, insults, or hostile language
- Trolling, inflammatory comments, or repeated disruptive behavior
- Dismissing others in a disrespectful or demeaning way
- Gatekeeping, exclusionary conduct, or unnecessary barriers to contribution
- Bad-faith arguments, deliberate misrepresentation, or repeated unconstructive conflict
- Publishing private, sensitive, or confidential information without permission
- Any conduct that makes collaboration unsafe, hostile, or unnecessarily difficult

## Community Principles

Terra Nova is guided by these principles:

- **Clarity over cleverness**
- **Automation over manual processes**
- **Consistency over fragmentation**
- **Quality and pragmatism in balance**
- **Open by default**

We encourage contributions that align with these principles and improve the ecosystem as a whole.

## Shared Ownership and Responsibility

Terra Nova is designed to support both speed and freedom without sacrificing responsibility.

We welcome new ideas, including ideas that introduce new directions. When a contribution expands the project in a way
that current maintainers are unsure about or do not wish to own directly, it may still be accepted — with ownership.

This means:

- contributors may be asked to take ongoing responsibility for the areas they introduce
- ownership should follow contribution
- innovation should not be blocked, but responsibility must remain clear
- long-term maintainability matters as much as initial implementation

Shared ownership is part of how Terra Nova stays open, practical, and sustainable.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all Terra Nova community spaces, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repositories
- issues
- pull requests
- discussions
- documentation
- project chat or community channels
- code reviews
- events or meetings associated with Terra Nova

It also applies when an individual is representing Terra Nova in public spaces.

## Reporting Concerns

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it to the maintainers through:

- GitHub private security reporting.

When reporting, please include as much relevant information as possible, such as:

- what happened
- where it happened
- when it happened
- who was involved
- any supporting context or evidence

All reports will be reviewed seriously and handled as promptly and fairly as possible.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct.

They may take any action they believe is appropriate in response to unacceptable behavior, including:

- private warning
- request to stop the behavior
- removal of content
- temporary restriction from participation
- permanent ban from some or all community spaces

Enforcement decisions will be made with the goal of protecting the health, safety, and long-term sustainability of the
community.

## Our Standards for Collaboration

Because Terra Nova is built around maintainable, collaborative software development, we expect contributors to support
healthy engineering practices, including when relevant:

- open discussion of trade-offs
- clear, reviewable changes
- respect for established quality standards
- documentation when behavior changes
- testing and automation where appropriate
- willingness to maintain what you introduce

Contributing code is important. Contributing responsibly is essential.

## Final Note

Terra Nova is open by design.

There is no lock-in, no unnecessary barriers, and no place for gatekeeping.

Bring your ideas.  
Build with care.  
Take ownership.  
Help others do the same.

Welcome to Terra Nova.
